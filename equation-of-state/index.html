Effective Equation of State

Using the TIGRESS-classic suite (Ostriker & Kim (2022)), we obtained a calibration for an effective equation of state -- a relation between the effective pressure and density Peff=ρσeff2P_{\rm eff}=\rho\sigma_{\rm eff}^2Peff​=ρσeff2​. We provided simple power-law fitting results using two characterizations. (1) The relation between the volume-weighted averages of the midplane total pressure and density of the warm and cold gas (Ptot,2pP_{\rm tot,2p}Ptot,2p​ and nH,2p=ρ2p/1.4mHn_{\rm H, 2p}=\rho_{\rm 2p}/1.4m_HnH,2p​=ρ2p​/1.4mH​ in Table 2 of OK22). (2) The relation between the mass-weighted vertical velocity dispersion of the warm and cold phase and the dynamical equilibrium pressure (σ‾eff,2p\overline{\sigma}_{\rm eff,2p}σeff,2p​ and PDEP_{\rm DE}PDE​ in Table 2 of OK22). From (1), we calculated the midplane effective velocity dispersion σeff,mid≡(Ptot,2p/ρ2p)1/2\sigma_{\rm eff,mid}\equiv(P_{\rm tot,2p}/\rho_{\rm 2p})^{1/2}σeff,mid​≡(Ptot,2p​/ρ2p​)1/2. From (2), we calculated the mass-weighted average density ρavg=PDE/σ‾eff,2p2\rho_{\rm avg}=P_{\rm DE}/\overline{\sigma}_{\rm eff,2p}^2ρavg​=PDE​/σeff,2p2​ and the press-density relation. Therefore, both provide same information; they just characterized differently.

In what follows, we will use PeffP_{\rm eff}Peff​ and nHn_HnH​ for effective pressure and number density. We do not distinguish Ptot,2pP_{\rm tot,2p}Ptot,2p​, PDEP_{\rm DE}PDE​ and Wtot\mathcal{W}_{\rm tot}Wtot​ as they are proven to be consistent with each other. We will use the nomenclature σ‾eff,2p→σeff,avg\overline{\sigma}_{\rm eff,2p}\to\sigma_{\rm eff,avg}σeff,2p​→σeff,avg​ to be consistent with the TIGRESS-NCR results.

Important

The power-law fit of the effective equation of state will not valid to the arbitrary low pressure and density. We show the fitting results and actual simulation data points. We caution that especially at low pressure and density regimes, the effective velocity dispersions cannot be much lower than the warm medium sound speed (∼10km/s\sim 10{\rm km/s}∼10km/s). It would be safe (and more physically meaningful) to apply a velocity floor of this level to the effective velocity dispersion and corresponding effective pressure when adopting this result. See this example.

TIGRESS-NCR calibration¶

Using the TIGRESS-NCR suite (Kim et al. (2024)), we obtained a calibration for σeff\sigma_{\rm eff}σeff​ as a function of the total pressure and gas metallicity, but the latter dependence is found to be very weak. So, here we omit the metallicity dependence.
