Feedback Yields

In the ISM, turbulent and thermal pressure support is quickly lost due to turbulence dissipation and cooling. In order to maintain the vertical dynamical equilibrium, there must exist efficient recovery of pressure support. Stellar feedback (esp. from massive young stars) is the major source of energy. Turbulence is mainly driven by SN feedback, and heating is dominated by the photoelectric effect of FUV radiation on grains. Assuming balance between gain and loss processes, one can define the feedback yield for each pressure component as a ratio between pressure and SFR surface density:

Υth,turb,mag=Pth,turb,magΣSFR\Upsilon_{\rm th, turb, mag} = \frac{P_{\rm th, turb, mag}}{\Sigma_{\rm SFR}}Υth,turb,mag​=ΣSFR​Pth,turb,mag​​
(1)

Taking the sum of pressures,

Ptot=Pth+Pturb+Pmag=(Υth+Υturb+Υmag)ΣSFR.P_{\rm tot} = P_{\rm th} + P_{\rm turb} + P_{\rm mag} = (\Upsilon_{\rm th} + \Upsilon_{\rm turb} + \Upsilon_{\rm mag})\Sigma_{\rm SFR}.Ptot​=Pth​+Pturb​+Pmag​=(Υth​+Υturb​+Υmag​)ΣSFR​.
(2)

We can then obtain the SFR surface density from the total weight Wtot\cal W_{\rm tot}Wtot​ and the calibrated values of Υ\UpsilonΥ, under the assumption that the total pressure PtotP_{\rm tot}Ptot​ is in balance with the total weight Wtot\cal W_{\rm tot}Wtot​.

An analytic form of the weight in vertical dynamical equilibrium is

PDE=πG2Σg2+[2G(ρsd)]1/2σeffP_{\rm DE} = \frac{\pi G}{2}\Sigma_g^2 + [2 G (\rho_{sd})]^{1/2} \sigma_\mathrm{eff}PDE​=2πG​Σg2​+[2G(ρsd​)]1/2σeff​
(3)

for Σg\Sigma_gΣg​ the gas surface density, ρsd\rho_{sd}ρsd​ the midplane star+dark matter midplane density, and σeff\sigma_\mathrm{eff}σeff​ the effective vertical velocity dispersion.

TIGRESS-classic calibration¶

The numerically calibrated total feedback yield from the TIGRESS-classic suite (Ostriker & Kim (2022); see Eqs (25)) is either

Υtot=1030km/s(PDE/kB104cm−3 K)−0.212\Upsilon_{\rm tot} = 1030{\rm km/s} \left(\frac{P_{\rm DE}/k_B}{10^4 {\rm cm^{-3}\,K}}\right)^{-0.212}Υtot​=1030km/s(104cm−3KPDE​/kB​​)−0.212
(4)

or the sum of thermal and turbulent+magnetic

Υth=267km/s(PDE/kB104cm−3 K)−0.506\Upsilon_{\rm th} = 267{\rm km/s} \left(\frac{P_{\rm DE}/k_B}{10^4 {\rm cm^{-3}\,K}}\right)^{-0.506}Υth​=267km/s(104cm−3KPDE​/kB​​)−0.506
(5)
Υturb+mag=1.5∗370km/s(PDE/kB104cm−3 K)−0.060\Upsilon_{\rm turb+mag} = 1.5*370{\rm km/s} \left(\frac{P_{\rm DE}/k_B}{10^4 {\rm cm^{-3}\,K}}\right)^{-0.060}Υturb+mag​=1.5∗370km/s(104cm−3KPDE​/kB​​)−0.060
(6)

where we assume that the magnetic contribution is 50% of the turbulent.
