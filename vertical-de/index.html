Vertical Dynamical Equilibrium

Basic formulation¶

The vertical dynamical equilibrium in a disk consisting of gas, stars, and dark matter can be written as

Ptot=ρgasσeff2=Σgas2Hgasσeff2=Wtot=Wg+W∗+Wd.P_{\rm tot} = \rho_{\rm gas} \sigma_{\rm eff}^2=\frac{\Sigma_{\rm gas}}{2 H_{\rm gas}} \sigma_{\rm eff}^2 = {\cal W}_\mathrm{tot}={\cal W}_\mathrm{g} + {\cal W}_* + {\cal W}_\mathrm{d}.Ptot​=ρgas​σeff2​=2Hgas​Σgas​​σeff2​=Wtot​=Wg​+W∗​+Wd​.
(1)

where

Wg=πGΣgas22,{\cal W}_\mathrm{g}= \frac{\pi G \Sigma_{\rm gas}^2}{2},Wg​=2πGΣgas2​​,
(2)
W∗≈πGΣgasΣ∗HgasHgas+H∗,{\cal W}_* \approx \pi G \Sigma_{\rm gas} \Sigma_* \frac{H_{\rm gas}}{H_{\rm gas} + H_*},W∗​≈πGΣgas​Σ∗​Hgas​+H∗​Hgas​​,
(3)

and

Wd=ζdΣgasΩd2Hgas{\cal W}_\mathrm{d} = \zeta_\mathrm{d} \Sigma_{\rm gas} \Omega_d^2 H_{\rm gas}Wd​=ζd​Σgas​Ωd2​Hgas​
(4)

are the weights (per unit area) of the gas in the disk within the gravitational potential of gas, stars, and dark matter, respectively.

Given Σgas\Sigma_{\rm gas}Σgas​, Σ∗\Sigma_*Σ∗​, H∗H_*H∗​, and Ωd\Omega_dΩd​, together with an assumption for σeff\sigma_{\rm eff}σeff​, we solve for HgasH_{\rm gas}Hgas​. We use a constant σeff\sigma_{\rm eff}σeff​ or a model for σeff\sigma_{\rm eff}σeff​ calibrated from numerical simulations.

Constant velocity dispersion¶

For given σeff\sigma_{\rm eff}σeff​, the equation for HgasH_{\rm gas}Hgas​ becomes a cubic. Using

Hgas−only=σeff2πGΣgasH_{\rm gas-only} = \frac{\sigma_{\rm eff}^2}{\pi G \Sigma_{\rm gas}}Hgas−only​=πGΣgas​σeff2​​
(5)

and

Hdm−only=σeff(2ζd)1/2Ωd,H_{\rm dm-only} = \frac{\sigma_{\rm eff}}{(2\zeta_d)^{1/2}\Omega_d},Hdm−only​=(2ζd​)1/2Ωd​σeff​​,
(6)

the dimensionless form of the equation can be written as

h3+[(1+2s∗)ηd2+η∗]h2+(η∗−1)ηd2h−η∗ηd2=0h^3 + [(1+2 s_*)\eta_d^2+\eta_*]h^2 + (\eta_*-1)\eta_d^2h -\eta_*\eta_d^2 = 0h3+[(1+2s∗​)ηd2​+η∗​]h2+(η∗​−1)ηd2​h−η∗​ηd2​=0

with h=Hgas/Hgas−onlyh = H_{\rm gas}/H_{\rm gas-only}h=Hgas​/Hgas−only​, s∗=Σ∗/Σgass_* = \Sigma_*/\Sigma_{\rm gas}s∗​=Σ∗​/Σgas​, η∗=H∗/Hgas−only\eta_* = H_*/H_{\rm gas-only}η∗​=H∗​/Hgas−only​, and ηd=Hdm−only/Hgas−only\eta_d = H_{\rm dm-only}/H_{\rm gas-only}ηd​=Hdm−only​/Hgas−only​. The only positive real solution of the cubic equation is

Hgas=2−Qcos⁡(θ3),θ=cos⁡−1(R−Q)H_{\rm gas} = 2\sqrt{-Q}\cos\left(\frac{\theta}{3}\right), \theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{R}{\sqrt{-Q}}\right)Hgas​=2−Q​cos(3θ​),θ=cos−1(−Q​R​)

where Q=(3a1−a22)/9Q = (3a_1-a_2^2)/9Q=(3a1​−a22​)/9 and R=(9a2a1−27a0−2a23)/54R=(9a_2a_1-27a_0-2a_2^3)/54R=(9a2​a1​−27a0​−2a23​)/54 with aia_iai​ for the coefficients of hih^ihi for i=0i=0i=0, 1, 2.

Important

In observations, a simplified weight formula has been widely used (e.g., Sun et al. (2020), Barrera-Ballesteros et al. (2021)):

Wtot≈πGΣgas22+Σgasσeff2Gρsd{\cal W}_{\rm tot}\approx \frac{\pi G \Sigma_{\rm gas}^2}{2} + \Sigma_{\rm gas}\sigma_{\rm eff}\sqrt{2 G\rho_{\rm sd}}Wtot​≈2πGΣgas2​​+Σgas​σeff​2Gρsd​​
(18)

where ρsd=ρ∗+ρdm\rho_{\rm sd} = \rho_* + \rho_{\rm dm}ρsd​=ρ∗​+ρdm​. This is equivalent to the limit of H∗≫HgasH_*\gg H_{\rm gas}H∗​≫Hgas​ with Σ∗>Σgas\Sigma_*>\Sigma_{\rm gas}Σ∗​>Σgas​ discussed above (2\sqrt{2}2​ instead of π\sqrt{\pi}π​ due to different assumptions in the vertical gas profiles, Gaussian vs exponential).

In practical applications, the relation between stellar disk scale length l∗l_*l∗​ and scale height z∗z_*z∗​ is often assumed; z∗=l∗/7.3z_*=l_*/7.3z∗​=l∗​/7.3 or ρ∗=Σ∗/(4z∗)=Σ∗/(0.54l∗)\rho_* = \Sigma_*/(4 z_*) = \Sigma_*/(0.54 l_*)ρ∗​=Σ∗​/(4z∗​)=Σ∗​/(0.54l∗​), which is based on the assumption of the isothermal (or sech2{\rm sech}^2sech2) stellar density profile. It is important to note that our definition of the stellar scale height yields H∗=2z∗H_*=2z_*H∗​=2z∗​. Therefore, when one wants to switch to the more general formula presented above using H∗H_*H∗​ explicitly, it is important to use the correct empirical relation consistent with the definition of H∗H_*H∗​, i.e.,

H∗=2z∗=2(l∗/7.3).H_* = 2 z_* = 2 (l_*/7.3).H∗​=2z∗​=2(l∗​/7.3).
